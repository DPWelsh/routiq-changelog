## The shape

Routiq's voice agent already handles inbound calls. Outbound is the natural extension: the AI calls patients to confirm appointments, follow up after a session, or re-engage someone who hasn't been in for a while.

The clinic owner sets the rules — who, when, what to say if they pick up, what to do if they don't. The AI does the calling, captures the outcome, updates the patient record.

## What we're working through

This is more product-design work than engineering work. Outbound voice can go wrong fast — patients don't expect a call from an AI, and timing matters. The hard parts are:

- Consent and compliance (the rules differ by region)
- Voice quality at scale (TTS that doesn't read as robotic)
- The handoff to a human when the call surfaces something complex
- Reporting back to the clinic in a way they actually look at

We're moving deliberately. Expect this on the roadmap for a while before it ships.
